MASSAGE FROM THE FOUNDER

I am happy to present 28th Annual Report of Akhil Bhartiya Antyodaya Sansthan. It has been a long journey towards promoting voices and credibility of the voluntary sectore among various stakeholders. Being credibie is simply the perception of being trustworthy and believable. The question about how NGOs develop and retain public trust and credibility has plagued many NGO activists over the last few decades. Akhil Bhartiya Antyodaya Sansthan has been pursuing its mandate to work towards building the stakeholder confidence and credibility of voluntary organizations through promotion of transparecy, Accountability and good governance practices. The spirit of Voluntary Organization to get accredited by Akhil Bhartiya Antyodaya Sansthan is based primarily on their commitment towards accountability and transparency which is further, enhanced by the Akhil Bhartiya Antyodaya Sansthan through various communication mechanisms.

This year Akhil Bhartiya Antyodaya Sansthan completes 28 years as a pioneer in the field of promoting Good Governance, Voluntary disclosure and Accreditation. This is a special year as there has been a substantial shift in the Accreditation scenario. It has moved from the space of building acceptance to gaining popularity due to the Medical Health, Education for poor children as well as the bubbling SR environment. The intention of Akhil Bhartiya Antyodaya Sansthan Alliance over the past one year has been to position itself as an expert on CSO Governance and Compliances, especially in the light of its experience of accrediting organizations over the past twelve years. The organizations' intention has also been to become the voluntary sector and the corporate sector and a knowledge hub in areas of Relief to poor person etc.

In the changing context lots of discussions and discourse is taking place around the concern for shrinking space for civil society. There is need that national and regional networks of voluntary organizations must take the accountability debate to their membership through a planned effort so as to strengthen their internal svstems and processes based on good practices. Together, it is possible to change the character, efficacy and relevance of the voluntary sector today in public perception while refocusing the need for a critical mass of credible organizations around the nation.
